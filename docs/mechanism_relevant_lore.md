# The Angmour Measurement System (AS) — in-game reference

The Angmour Measurement System (AS) is the standard measure across the Simple RL realm. It grew from the old Imperial hands and the old Babylonian counting practices, and it was adopted by guilds, road-surveyors, and artisans because it blends human scale, clean divisibility, and a single small micro-unit for fine work.

Mechanically, AS is built two ways at once: a **decimal craft scale** for everyday making (10 halflines = 1 thumb) and a **sexagesimal micro-point** for specialist work (60 points = 1 thumb). For travel and surveying the ladder rises in a deliberate sequence of ×5/×6/×10 steps so that body measurements, lengths, and land all tessellate cleanly.

**External anchor (exact):**
**1 AS thumb = 11/12 Imperial inch**

## 1) Core Definitions & Linear Scale

The system distinguishes a micro/craft scale for artisans and a body/travel scale used by field workers and road crews. The tables below are the canonical in-game reference players and NPCs use.

| Unit        | Ratio (AS)               | AS Thumbs | AS Points | Imperial Equivalent (exact) |
| :---------- | :----------------------- | --------: | --------: | :-------------------------- |
| AS Point    | Base micro-unit          |      1/60 |         1 | 11/720 in                   |
| AS Halfline | 6 Points                 |      1/10 |         6 | 11/120 in                   |

| AS Thumb    | 10 Halflines = 60 Points |         1 |        60 | 11/12 in                    |

**In-game note (craftsmen).** Guild bench rulers show both halflines and points so journeymen can work to whole-number divisions that mesh with guild formulae and merchant marks.

**In-game note (fieldwork).** Road markers and boundary stones are set in Chains and Kos. A surveyor’s chain is treated as the primary linear unit for maps and taxation.

## 2) Land and Area (The Surveyor’s Grid)

All land measures are defined in **AS Paces** and align to the Chain (which is 60 paces). This makes square chains and acres the natural tiles for fields, estates, and map grids.

**Key identity:**
**1 AS Acre = 60 × 60 AS Paces = 1 Square Chain**

| Unit        | AS Dimensions               | AS Sq Paces | Count per Sq Danna |
| :---------- | :-------------------------- | ----------: | -----------------: |
| AS Sq Perch | 1 × 1 AS Perch              |          36 |             90,000 |
| AS Rood     | 25 AS Sq Perches            |         900 |              3,600 |
| AS Acre     | 4 AS Roods = 1 Square Chain |       3,600 |                900 |
| AS Yardland | 30 AS Acres                 |     108,000 |                 30 |
| AS Hide     | 6 AS Yardlands              |     648,000 |                  5 |
| AS Sq Danna | 1,800 × 1,800 AS Paces      |   3,240,000 |                  1 |

**Square Danna layout in chains:**

* 1 Danna = 1,800 paces = 30 chains.
* **1 Square Danna = 30 × 30 chains = 900 square chains = 900 AS acres.**

**In-game note (mapping & economy).** A Danna is the unit used for regional maps, lordly tithes, and long-term land grants. Smaller holdings are priced in Acres and Yardlands, while military logistics and patrol routes use Chains and Kos.

## 3) Thematic Properties

### 3.1 The 30-minute Danna

The system ties distance to human motion. At the standard walking rate used by the realm’s road keepers (defined for gameplay and travel estimation), a traveler moving steadily at **1 AS pace per second** covers:

* 1 Chain (60 paces) in **1 minute**.
* 1 Kos (6 chains) in **6 minutes**.
* 1 Danna (30 chains) in **30 minutes** (1,800 seconds).

**In-game note (travel & timers).** Use the Danna as a natural time block for travel events, foraging checks, and watch shifts. A messenger’s allowable distance in a single watch is often expressed in Danna.

### 3.2 Angmour-style divisibility

The ladder alternates cleanly through **×5 and ×6** so each step factors simply for surveying and craft standards:

* Hand → Pace (×5)
* Pace → Perch (×6)
* Perch → Chain (×10 = 2×5)
* Chain → Kos (×6)
* Kos → Danna (×5)

**In-game note (procedures).** Surveyors and architects favour this ladder because it yields integer subdivisions at each practical scale: body, road, and land.

### 3.3 The artisan point (specialist micro-scale)

* 1 AS thumb = **60 points**.
* 1 Halfline = **6 points**.
* 1 Line = **12 points**.

This single micro-point lets specialists subdivide thumbs into whole numbers compatible with **5/6/10/12/60** without changing the main craft units.

**In-game note (craft & trade).** Jewelers, instrument-makers, and the royal mint mark their gauges in points and halflines. Contract work is often written in whole points so measurements remain precise in trade disputes.

## 4) Work & Verification

### 4.1 Linear verification

* 1 Hand = 12 thumbs.
* 1 Pace = 5 hands = 60 thumbs.
* 1 Chain = 60 paces.
* 1 Danna = 30 chains = 30 × 60 paces = **1,800 paces**.

**In-game note (checks).** A field crew verifies a chain by laying out 60 measured paces and then checking by hand and palm standards; that routine is how boundary stones are certified.

### 4.2 Area verification

* Square Danna: 1,800 × 1,800 = **3,240,000 sq paces**.
* Acres per Square Danna: 3,240,000 / 3,600 = **900**.
* Yardlands per Square Danna: 900 / 30 = **30**.
* Hides per Square Danna: 30 / 6 = **5**.

**In-game note (tallies).** Tax rolls and harvest forecasts are computed in Acres and Yardlands; regional levies refer to Hides and the Square Danna for major allocations.

### 4.3 Imperial bridge identities (exact)

With the canonical bridge value **1 AS thumb = 11/12 Imperial inch** the principal Imperial equivalents are:

* 1 AS Hand = **11 Imperial inches**.
* 1 AS Pace = **55 Imperial inches**.
* 1 AS Chain = **275 Imperial feet**.
* 1 Imperial mile = **1,152 AS paces**.

**In-game note (trade & diplomacy).** Merchant contracts that cross borders specify both AS and Imperial measures and always cite the 11/12 anchor so guild clerks can convert prices and tolls exactly.

## Quick-use summary (for players)

* Use **Points/Halflines/Lines/Thumbs** at the bench for crafting and fine work.
* Use **Hands/Paces/Chains** for roadwork, signs, and immediate travel distances.
* Use **Kos/Dannas** for regional travel and time-based events; a Danna equals **30 minutes** at the standard walking pace.
* Use **Square Danna / Acres / Yardlands** for estates, taxes, and map tiles.
